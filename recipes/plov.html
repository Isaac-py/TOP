Plov

Description

This recipe describes a traditional Uzbek plov made with aromatic devzira rice, beef, and spices. The cooking process begins with frying the meat until golden brown, followed by adding onions and carrots to create the base of the dish—zirvak. Once the spices, including cumin, garlic, and hot red pepper, are added, the mixture is simmered for about 40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

After that, the thoroughly washed and soaked rice is carefully layered over the zirvak, covered with water, and cooked until all the liquid is absorbed. The plov is then left to steam under a lid, ensuring a fluffy texture and rich aroma. Before serving, it is garnished with fresh green onions, chili peppers, and pomegranate seeds, with quail eggs added for a finishing touch. This dish offers a perfect balance of flavors, spices, and an elegant presentation.

Ingredients

  • 600g devzira (Uzgen-Özgön) rice
  • 100g cottonseed oil (can be substituted with sunflower oil)
  • 650g beef brisket
  • 300g onion
  • 300g yellow carrot (or substitute with orange carrot)
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 red hot chili pepper
  • 2 teaspoons cumin (zira)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon black pepper
  • 8-10 quail eggs
  • Green onions, pomegranate seeds, chili pepper

Steps

  1. Cut the meat into small cubes. Slice the onion into half-rings. Julienne the carrot.
  2. Heat the oil in a kazan (cauldron) until hot.
  3. Add the meat and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add the onions and sauté until soft.
  5. Add the carrots and continue frying until they soften.
  6. Season with salt, cumin, and black pepper.
  7. Pour in water until it covers the zirvak by about a finger’s width.
  8. Add the garlic and red chili pepper.
  9. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 40 minutes.
  10. Rinse the rice under warm running water until the water runs clear.
  11. Soak the rice in lightly salted water for 40 minutes.
  12. Remove the red chili pepper and garlic from the zirvak.
  13. Evenly layer the rice on top of the zirvak.
  14. Pour in enough water to cover the rice by 1-1.5 fingers.
  15. Cook on high heat until the rice absorbs the water.
  16. Once most of the water has been absorbed, reduce the heat to low.
  17. Cover the kazan with a lid and let the rice steam.
  18. Stir occasionally until the rice is fully cooked.
  19. Return the garlic and red chili pepper to the kazan.
  20. Turn off the heat and let the plov rest for 10 minutes.
  21. Chop the green onions and chili pepper.
  22. Mix the chopped ingredients with pomegranate seeds.
  23. Serve the plov garnished with the green onion mixture.
  24. Decorate with quail eggs before serving.
